07How to Read Capital Flow
Concentrated inflow and concentrated consolidation are different regimes that require different watchlist logic. In concentrated inflow (05.26–05.27), time-lagged spread within the leading cluster is the dominant rotation pattern. In concentrated consolidation (05.28–05.30), fresh cluster formation from an unrelated axis is more likely than spread within the existing chain. The capital-flow state shift on 05.28 was the signal to change watchlist logic — not just to add caution.
A cluster's capital share trajectory is more informative than its single-day reading. The AI infrastructure cluster's share declined from approximately 38% to 30% over the week, but it never exited — it rotated internally. The defense cluster's share declined from 12% to 7% and its internal breadth collapsed. These trajectories told different stories about structural durability that single-day readings obscured.
The overheating-management zone is not a binary signal — it is a deepening process. Optical Communications was flagged as overheated on 05.26 (3-day surge +25%), again on 05.27 (3-day surge +28%), and corrected sharply on 05.29. The deepening of the chase zone across consecutive sessions was the signal that the correction risk was accumulating, not just present.
Event-driven breakouts in concentrated-consolidation environments have a characteristic signature: exceptional volume on day one, reversal or fade on day two. IT Services (05.28 breakout, 05.29 reversal), Battery/ESS (05.29 breakout, 05.30 fade), and the defense cluster's 05.26–05.27 confirmation followed by 05.28 moderation all fit this pattern. The exception — IT Services re-extending on 05.30 — is the signal worth watching most carefully.
The KOSPI/KOSDAQ divergence is a capital-concentration risk indicator, not just a market breadth reading. When KOSPI outperforms KOSDAQ by 3–6 percentage points in a single session, it signals that capital is concentrating in large-cap domestic names rather than broadening — which compresses the window between signal and overheating for any new breakout.
